Entry One — The Birthday That Wasn't

The story begins on the day of Pedro's birthday.

Floyy was standing beside Denish, holding her latest novel.

He smiled and extended the book toward her.

"Can I have your autograph?"

Denish signed it.

Floyy admired the signature.

"Your signature is beautiful..."

"Just like your novels."

Denish laughed softly.

"Peter doesn't care about any of my books."

Just then... Marissa arrived carrying snacks for both of them.

Denish smiled.

"So... where's the hero of this house?"

"Dad?"

Marissa sighed.

"He's gone to Florence's funeral."

"I don't know... he'll probably return by evening."

Denish quietly replied,

"He should've gone."

"At least someone's giving her a proper farewell."

Marissa looked down.

"I wanted to go too."

"But your father didn't allow me."

At that very moment... Peter slowly walked downstairs.

Denish smiled.

"Look who's finally awake."

"The prince of the house."

"You'd sleep forever if someone let you."

Marissa laughed.

"He only came downstairs because you're here."

"Otherwise... he barely leaves his room."

She looked toward Floyy.

"Ever since childhood..."

"The only person Peter truly listens to..."

"...is his sister."

Everyone smiled.

After a while... Peter and Denish walked outside, teasing each other.

Floyy quietly followed them.

He hid behind a bookshelf near the hallway and listened.

Entry Two — Behind the Bookshelf

Denish looked at Peter.

"Today's Dad's birthday."

"And instead of celebrating..."

"He's attending Florence's funeral."

"Maybe... he finally regrets betraying Mom."

Floyy's Thoughts

So Denish already knows about the affair...

Peter slowly replied,

"It's a good thing Mom doesn't know."

Denish looked at him.

"But I think she deserves to know."

"How long has Dad been hiding all this behind the name of literature?"

Peter immediately shook his head.

"Mom is too innocent."

"She won't survive this."

"You remember... a few years ago..."

"She tried to kill herself."

"It took everything just to save her."

"We can't tell her."

Denish lowered her head.

"But it's still unbelievable."

"How could Dad have an affair..."

"...with someone who worked in our own house?"

She clenched her fists.

"He's disgusting."

"And then... that accident."

Peter slowly looked at her.

"It wasn't an accident."

Denish frowned.

"What?"

Peter's voice trembled.

"Florence didn't die in an accident."

"...Dad killed her."

— Silence —

Denish stared at him.

"...What did you just say?"

Just then... Marissa's voice echoed from inside the house.

"Peter! Denish! Dinner is ready!"

Seeing Marissa approaching... Floyy quickly stepped away before anyone noticed him.

Marissa looked around.

"Floyy... have you seen them?"

He calmly replied,

"No. I haven't."

Marissa smiled.

"Then help me with the cake."

"I still have a lot of work left."

Entry Three — The Garage

A little later... Floyy noticed Peter and Denish talking again.

This time... inside the garage.

He quietly moved closer and listened once again.

Denish spoke first.

"Maybe you're mistaken."

"Dad can't do something like that."

Peter looked directly into her eyes.

"I saw everything with my own eyes."

From Floyy's Assignment

Unfortunately... before I could get any closer... their conversation had almost ended.

But I still managed to hear something important.

Denish looked at Peter.

"You've spent too much time playing video games."

"You can't even separate reality from imagination anymore."

Peter replied,

"After the accident... I secretly followed them."

Denish remained silent. Then slowly spoke,

"Fine. Let's assume you're telling the truth."

"Then this isn't just an affair anymore."

"It's murder."

"And if this comes out..."

"Dad's reputation... my career... everything..."

"...will be destroyed."

She looked at him seriously.

"Have you told anyone?"

"Did you tell Floyy?"

Peter immediately shook his head.

"Why would I?"

"You're the only person I've told."

Denish took a deep breath.

"Good. Then keep your mouth shut."

"It's a blessing the police haven't looked at this angle yet."

Peter suddenly broke down in tears.

Without warning... Denish slapped him hard across the face.

"Stop being weak."

"And listen carefully."

"If you don't do exactly as I say..."

"I'll kill you myself."

"Do you understand?"

Floyy's Assignment — Closing Lines

Peter locked himself inside his room again.

He didn't even come outside to see Denish off.

Entry Four — The Library, Once More

Professor Jenn slowly closed the notebook.

Floyy looked at him.

"Sir... are you alright?"

Jenn remained silent. Then softly replied,

"I don't understand anything anymore."

Floyy sighed.

"That house isn't the same anymore."

"I don't know how many more secrets are hidden inside those walls."

Jenn looked outside the library window.

"We've come this far... and yet..."

"We still don't have a single solid piece of evidence."

Floyy quietly replied,

"But Peter definitely knows something."

He paused.

"How did Peter reach Hotel Bellavista?"

"And... what exactly did he see at the hospital?"

Jenn nodded.

"And now... even his sister has silenced him."

Floyy slowly spoke.

"I think... we'll have to involve Peter's mother."

Jenn immediately replied,

"Wren Marissa?"

Floyy froze.

"...Wren Marissa?"

"You already knew Peter's mother?"

Jenn realized what he had said.

He looked away.

"...Forget that."

"What matters now... is what we do next."

Floyy quietly answered,

"We have no choice."

"Denish won't tell us anything."

"And I don't think Peter will either."

"If we tell Marissa even a small part of the truth..."

"Maybe she'll start asking Peter questions."

Jenn immediately shook his head.

"Absolutely not."

"You heard Denish."

"She threatened Peter."

"And... the last time Marissa found herself trapped..."

"...she tried to end her own life."

Floyy looked directly into Jenn's eyes.

"If you're so worried about Peter's mother..."

"...then who is she to you?"

Jenn suddenly lost his temper.

"She is someone I know!"

"We studied together in college."

"There. Are you happy now?"

— Silence Filled the Library —

A few seconds later... Jenn took a deep breath.

"We'll talk about this another day."

"For now... write everything you've learned."

"And submit your assignment tomorrow."

The investigation had reached a point...

...where every answer created two new questions.

The Investigation Continues...

A slap in the garage. A threat spoken in the family's own voice.
And a name — Wren Marissa — that Jenn never meant to say out loud.
Every bloodline in this house is hiding something.
