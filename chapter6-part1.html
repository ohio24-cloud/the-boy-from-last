Chapter 6 — The Housemaid (Part I)
Chapter Theme Whispers Before the Storm

Chapter Six · Part One

The Housemaid

"It felt like the entire novel was actually written about... only one woman."

✦Psychological Fiction

✦Secrets / Old Wounds / Buried Confessions

I

Professor Jenn once again invited Floyy to his house to read his latest assignment together. Like every week, Jenn sat on the sofa with a printed copy of Floyy's story while Floyy quietly waited beside him.

Jenn adjusted the papers in his hands.

"Let's see what you've written this time."

He began reading.

Life inside Peter's house had become peaceful... most of the time.

There was also a housemaid named Florence, around twenty-eight years old. She only came four days a week — and even then, only whenever she felt like showing up.

For some reason... she loved making my life difficult. She never kept my clothes where they belonged. Whenever she entered my room, she would tease me — "You used to live in a tiny rented room... now look at you, living in a mansion."

One afternoon, while Peter and I were studying together, she accidentally spilled juice all over me.

She constantly got scolded by Marissa because she would smoke cigarettes and throw the cigarette butts into her flower garden. Marissa would always complain, "You're ruining my flowers!"

But despite all that... Florence was actually a very good cook.

That evening, Mr. Pedro returned home from work. As usual, he and Marissa started talking downstairs. I happened to be standing near the staircase and overheard their conversation.

Marissa sighed. "Florence was impossible today. She threw another cigarette into my flower garden." Pedro smiled. "You scolded her, didn't you?" "Of course I did." Pedro laughed softly. "Then she'll probably remember it next time."

The two of them smiled at each other before walking into their bedroom.

II

Professor Jenn suddenly stopped reading.

He slowly lowered the papers and looked at Floyy.

"Wait... were you seriously standing outside their room listening to their conversation?"

Floyy simply looked at him... and gave him a faint mischievous smile.

Jenn shook his head. "Don't you have any shame?"

Floyy only smiled wider.

Jenn sighed before continuing.

A little later, Selen called both of them to the dining table. She had ordered dinner from outside. The three of them quietly started eating together.

During dinner, Floyy suddenly spoke.

"Professor... I recently read your very first novel..."

Jenn smiled proudly. "'12 Women'?"

Floyy nodded. "Yes."

Jenn leaned back confidently. "So... what did you think?"

Floyy replied calmly, "It was interesting." He paused for a moment. Then continued, "But while reading it... I noticed something."

Jenn looked at him curiously.

"The book wasn't really about twelve different women."

A brief silence filled the dining table.

Floyy continued. "It felt like the entire novel was actually written about... only one woman."

Jenn's smile disappeared instantly. "Don't talk nonsense."

Across the table, Selen slowly turned toward Jenn. She didn't say a single word. But the expression in her eyes was enough to make Jenn uncomfortable.

III

A few moments later, Jenn quietly stood up and walked into his room. Floyy and Selen continued eating together.

Unknown to them, Professor Jenn had quietly stopped behind his bedroom door. He remained there... listening.

Of course, both Floyy and Selen already knew he was standing there.

Floyy smiled softly. Then looked toward Selen.

"You're very beautiful."

Selen laughed awkwardly.

Floyy continued, "Professor Jenn never compliments you, does he?"

Selen smiled. But there was sadness hidden inside that smile.

"He's always been like that."

A little while later, Floyy left Jenn's house and returned to Peter's home.

That night, Jenn and Selen stepped outside together to throw away the household garbage. After returning, the two of them sat down on the living room sofa.

The silence lasted for a while.

Jenn finally spoke. "Are you free tomorrow?"

Selen looked at him. "Why?"

Jenn smiled faintly. "I was thinking... maybe we could go somewhere together." "Or perhaps watch a movie."

For the first time that evening, a gentle smile appeared on Selen's face. "Why not?"

Jenn smiled back.

IV

The Next Day.

That afternoon, the school welcomed a special guest. A renowned novelist... Pedro.

Before the event began, Professor Steve approached Jenn.

"Professor Jenn, today's questionnaire session will be conducted by you."

Jenn looked surprised. "Me? Why me?"

Steve smiled. "Because you've known him for years." "Who could interview him better than you?"

Jenn hesitated. "I'll... try."

The program concluded successfully. Later, Professor Steve, his wife, Selen, Jenn and Pedro gathered together for lunch. The conversation remained light.

Until Steve suddenly smiled. "Pedro... congratulations once again."

Pedro looked at him. "For what?"

Steve laughed. "Your daughter's novel." "I heard it has already been nominated for a literary award."

Pedro smiled proudly. "She's worked very hard for it."

Jenn quietly listened. He had never heard Pedro speak about his family before.

Across the table, Selen noticed Jenn had suddenly become unusually quiet. She looked at him with sympathy.

Jenn slowly stood up. "Excuse me..."

Without saying another word, he quietly walked toward the washroom.

V

That evening, Jenn and Selen returned home.

As they entered the house, Selen gently closed the door behind them. The silence between them returned once again.

Then, without looking at him, she quietly asked, "So..."

"Was your first novel..."

She finally turned toward Jenn.

"...really written about the woman who was your first love?"

Jenn froze.
