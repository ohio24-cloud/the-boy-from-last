Entry One — Hotel Bellavista

Floyy stood outside Hotel Bellavista.

Professor Jenn's final words echoed inside his head.

"Find out what really happened that night."

He took a deep breath... and walked inside.

The hotel looked exactly as it had on the night of Florence's accident.

Luxurious. Beautiful. Silent. Almost... Too silent.

Floyy walked toward the reception desk. Taking Pedro's photograph from his pocket... He gently placed it on the counter.

"Excuse me..." "Can you tell me anything about this man?"

The receptionist looked down. For just a second... Her expression froze. Then... As if nothing had happened... She slid the photograph back.

"I'm sorry, sir." "We're not allowed to share any information regarding our guests."

Her voice sounded professional. But... Floyy had already noticed something. She recognized him. Yet... She deliberately chose to stay silent.

Floyy quietly nodded. "I understand." He left the reception. But one question remained inside his mind. Why was she afraid?

The Search

He searched almost every floor. Restaurant. Lobby. Conference Hall. No one seemed willing to talk.

Eventually... He reached the hotel's laundry department. Workers remained busy folding towels and bedsheets.

Floyy once again showed Pedro's photograph. "Have you ever seen this man?" Everyone shook their heads. "No." "I don't know him." Some didn't even bother looking at the picture.

Disappointed... Floyy turned around. Just then—

"Sir." "You aren't allowed to enter this area."

A security guard approached him. Floyy immediately apologized. "I'm sorry." "I'll leave." The guard pointed toward another corridor. "The exit is that way."

Floyy nodded and began walking. Then... The guard suddenly looked at him again.

"...Floyy?"

Floyy stopped. Slowly... He turned around. "...How do you know my name?"

The guard smiled. The moment Floyy saw his face... His eyes widened.

"...Jessic?!"

Jessic laughed. "Took you long enough."

The two shook hands. "What are you doing here?" Floyy asked.

Jessic scratched the back of his head. "What else?" "I couldn't clear the Civil Service exam." "So..." "I became a security guard." He laughed. "But forget me." "You look different." "What are you doing now?"

Floyy smiled. "I'm studying Electrical Engineering at Madrid High School."

Jessic gently punched his shoulder. "Not bad." "You finally escaped construction work." Both of them laughed. For a brief moment... It felt like old times.

Jessic suddenly noticed the photograph still in Floyy's hand. He frowned. "Who's that?"

Floyy handed him the picture. "I'm looking for information about this man."

Jessic looked carefully. Then nodded. "Oh..." "I know him." "He comes here quite often." "He's a famous novelist." "But..." "What do you want with him?"

Floyy sighed. "It's a long story." "And nobody inside this hotel wants to answer my questions."

Jessic smiled. "Then..." "You found the right person." "I know more about him than most people working here."

Floyy looked surprised. "Seriously?"

Jessic nodded. "Come with me."

Entry Two — The Rooftop Revelation

The two climbed onto the rooftop. The city lights stretched endlessly beneath them.

Jessic lit a cigarette. The silence lasted a few moments. Then... He finally spoke.

"You know..." "I witnessed yesterday's accident."

Floyy immediately looked at him. "...What?"

Jessic nodded slowly. "My shift had just ended." "I came up here to smoke." "And then..." "I saw them." "Pedro..." "...and Florence." "They were arguing."

Floyy frowned. "What kind of argument?"

Jessic looked toward the rooftop floor. "I couldn't hear everything." "But..." "I knew something wasn't right."

Floyy remained silent. Jessic continued.

"You asked who Pedro is?" "Every month..." "He books the hotel's most expensive suite." "But here's the strange part..." "He always orders..." "...the most expensive wine..." "...and the cheapest ice cream."

Floyy looked confused. "...Ice cream?"

Jessic laughed. "Yeah." "And every time I delivered room service..." "I'd find different women's clothes inside." He shrugged. "Maybe he thought nobody noticed."

Floyy looked disturbed. "But..." "He's married."

Jessic smiled bitterly. "People like him..." "Look respectable." "But appearances..." "...are the easiest lies to believe."

Both of them laughed quietly. The laughter disappeared almost immediately.

Floyy looked toward Jessic once again. "So..." "Were they doing the same thing yesterday?"

Jessic slowly shook his head. "No." "Yesterday..." "Was different." "Very different."

He threw the cigarette onto the rooftop. Crushed it beneath his shoe. Then... Looked straight into Floyy's eyes.

"I wasn't trying to listen..." "But..." "The voices coming from that room..." "...weren't normal."

Floyy's heartbeat suddenly became louder. "...What kind of voices?"

Jessic remained silent for several seconds. The wind grew stronger. Finally... He whispered...

"...The kind..." "...that no one should ever have to hear."

The Truth Deepens...

A witness to the silence. A confession beneath city lights. And a secret
that ties a literary master to the night Florence fell.

What happened in that room?
And what did Jessic truly hear?
