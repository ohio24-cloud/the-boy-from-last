Entry One — Hotel Bellavista

Floyy stood outside Hotel Bellavista.

Professor Jenn's final words echoed repeatedly inside his mind.

"Find out what really happened that night."

Taking a deep breath... He pushed open the revolving glass door.

The luxurious lobby looked exactly the same as it had on the night of Florence's accident.

Elegant. Beautiful. Silent. Almost... Too silent.

Floyy slowly walked toward the reception desk. Taking Pedro's photograph from his pocket... He gently placed it on the counter.

"Excuse me..." "Could you tell me something about this man?"

The receptionist looked down at the photograph. For just a brief second... Her expression froze. Then... Almost instantly... She forced a professional smile.

"I'm sorry, sir." "We're not allowed to share any information regarding our guests." She slowly returned the photograph.

Floyy quietly nodded. "...I understand." He walked away. But before leaving... He glanced back one last time.

The receptionist was still staring at Pedro's photograph. She recognized him. Yet... She deliberately chose to remain silent.

The Search

Floyy spent nearly an hour searching throughout the hotel. The restaurant. The conference hall. The lobby. Housekeeping.

Every answer remained the same. "I don't know." "I'm sorry." "I can't help you."

Eventually... Without realizing it... He entered the hotel's laundry department. Workers remained busy folding towels and bedsheets.

Floyy once again showed Pedro's photograph. "Have you ever seen this man before?"

Everyone shook their heads. "No." "I've never seen him." Some of them didn't even bother looking.

Disappointed... Floyy turned around. Just then—

"Sir." "You aren't allowed to enter this area."

A security guard approached him. Floyy immediately apologized. "I'm sorry." "I'll leave." The guard pointed toward another corridor. "The exit is that way."

Floyy nodded. As he began walking... The guard suddenly looked at him again.

"...Floyy?"

Floyy stopped. Slowly... He turned around. "...How do you know my name?"

The guard smiled. The moment Floyy saw his face... His eyes widened.

"...Jessic?!"

Jessic burst into laughter. "Took you long enough."

The two shook hands warmly. "What are you doing here?" Floyy asked.

Jessic scratched the back of his head. "What else?" "I couldn't clear the Civil Service examination." "So..." "I became a security guard." He laughed. "But enough about me." "What are you doing these days?"

Floyy smiled. "I'm studying Electrical Engineering."

Jessic gently punched his shoulder. "Not bad." "You've come a long way from carrying cement bags at that construction site." Both of them laughed.

For a brief moment... It felt like the old days.

Jessic suddenly noticed the photograph still resting inside Floyy's hand. He frowned. "Who's that?"

Floyy handed him the picture. "I'm looking for information about this man."

Jessic looked carefully. Then nodded. "Oh..." "I know him." "He visits this hotel quite often." "He's a famous novelist." "But..." "What do you want with him?"

Floyy sighed. "It's a long story." "And no one inside this hotel is willing to answer my questions."

Jessic smiled. "Then you've come to the right person." "I probably know more about him than most people working here."

Floyy looked surprised. "Seriously?"

Jessic nodded. "Come."

Entry Two — The Room Confession

The two climbed toward the rooftop. The evening breeze quietly swept across the city.

Jessic leaned against the railing. Then lit a cigarette.

For a few moments... Neither of them spoke.

Finally... Jessic broke the silence.

"You know..." "I witnessed yesterday's accident."

Floyy immediately looked at him. "...What?"

Jessic slowly nodded. "My shift had just ended." "I came up here to smoke." "And then..." "I saw them." "Pedro..." "...and Florence." "They were standing on this very rooftop." "They were arguing."

Floyy frowned. "What kind of argument?"

"I couldn't hear everything, but later I heard them arguing from inside the room."

Florence
"Go ahead..." "Let's see what you can actually do." "I'm not afraid of your threats anymore." "You bastard." "Do whatever you want." "I'll expose you in front of everyone." "I'll tell them who you really are." "I have proof."

Silence.

Floyy looked toward Jessic. "So..." "They were arguing?"

Jessic slowly shook his head. "No..." "It wasn't just an argument." "It sounded like..." "...they were threatening each other."

Floyy remained silent. After a few moments... He quietly asked, "What was happening inside the room?"

Jessic shrugged helplessly. "I don't know." "I never got the chance to go inside." "I was still outside..." "Listening from the corridor."

Just Then

RING... RING...

Jessic's phone suddenly started vibrating. He looked at the screen. "Yeah..." "I'm coming." He disconnected the call.

Then looked back at Floyy. "Sorry, brother." "I have to get back to work."

Floyy smiled. "It's okay." "I've already taken enough of your time."

Jessic laughed. "No, man." "It actually felt good meeting you again." He began walking away. Then suddenly stopped.

"Oh..." "I almost forgot."

Floyy looked at him. "What?"

Jessic scratched his head. "Yesterday..." "A boy around your age came here." "He was asking the exact same questions."

Floyy frowned. "...Same questions?"

Jessic nodded. "He wanted to know what happened before Florence fell." "Just like you."

Floyy remained silent. Jessic continued.

"But then..." "He asked me something strange."

Floyy looked confused. "What?"

Jessic looked directly into Floyy's eyes. "He asked me..."

'Do you think it was really an accident...' 'Or was Florence murdered?'

Floyy froze. "...What?" "Murder?"

Jessic slowly nodded. "At first..." "I was shocked too." "I saw the accident with my own eyes." "So I ignored it." "But after he left..." "I kept thinking."

He looked toward the rooftop. "There was something wrong."

Floyy quietly listened. Jessic continued.

"How did he know Pedro and Florence were arguing?" "I never told him that." "So..." "How could he suddenly start talking about murder?"

Silence. Neither of them spoke. Only the sound of the wind remained.

Entry Three — The Investigation Deepens

Far Away — Inside Professor Jenn's Study

Professor Jenn silently read every single line of Floyy's assignment. His eyes stopped at one word.

Murder.

Jenn slowly whispered to himself... "...Murder?"

He immediately stood up. Without wasting another second... He grabbed his phone. Dialed Floyy.

The call connected. "Floyy!" "I need you to go back to that hotel." "Right now." "If there's even the slightest chance..." "...that Florence was murdered..." "Then we need proof." "CCTV." "Security footage." "Anything." "Search everything."

Floyy remained silent. Then calmly replied, "Professor..." "Please calm down." "I was actually going to tell you this tomorrow."

Jenn frowned. "...Tell me what?"

Floyy took a deep breath. Then quietly said...

"...I think..." "...I know who that boy was."

Jenn immediately stood up. His voice trembled. "...What did you just say?" "You know..." "...who that boy was?"
