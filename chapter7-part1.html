Entry One — The Assignment Resumes

The next morning... everything appeared normal.

Professor Jenn continued teaching his literature class. Students quietly filled their seats. As always... Floyy sat beside the window. The same seat. The same notebook. The same expression.

Only... Jenn wasn't the same anymore.

The lecture ended. Students slowly walked out of the classroom. Before Floyy could leave— Jenn called him.

"Floyy." He stopped. Jenn looked directly into his eyes.

"...Your extra classes." "We're starting them again."

Floyy looked completely confused. "Sir...?" "But yesterday..." "...you said there wouldn't be any more classes."

Jenn picked up his files without looking at him. "Forget yesterday." "My cabin." "This evening." Without waiting for a reply... he walked away.

Evening

A soft knock echoed through Professor Jenn's cabin. "Come in." Floyy entered. The room smelled of old books and fresh coffee.

Jenn remained silent for a few seconds. Then finally asked—

"So..." "Tell me." "What happened inside Peter's house after Florence's accident?" "And..." "...how is Mr. Pedro?"

Floyy looked at him carefully. A strange smile appeared. "Professor..." "Why are you suddenly so interested?" "...especially in Mr. Pedro?" He paused. "...Did you already know him?"

Jenn had just taken a sip of coffee. The question caught him completely off guard. He suddenly began coughing violently. Coffee almost spilled across the table.

Floyy quietly waited. Jenn finally calmed himself. "...Just answer."

Floyy slowly opened his notebook.

Witness Account — Floyy's Notebook, transcribed

It happened this morning. We were having breakfast. Suddenly... the phone rang. Mrs. Merissa answered it. Her face slowly changed.

"Hello...?" "Yes..." "This is Mr. Pedro's residence."

A voice from the other side replied— "This is the police station." "We're sorry to inform you..." "...Florence passed away this morning."

Merissa froze. "...What?" The phone slipped from her hands.

Pedro immediately picked it up. "This is Pedro speaking." The officer repeated the news. "We couldn't find any of Florence's relatives." "So we contacted the last registered address."

Before Pedro could answer— Peter... who had been quietly eating breakfast... suddenly collapsed onto the floor. He couldn't breathe. His entire body began shaking.

"P-Peter!" Merissa rushed toward him. Floyy immediately helped lift him. Together... Pedro and Merissa carried Peter into his bedroom. Floyy remained standing outside. The bedroom door wasn't fully closed. He could hear everything.

Merissa's voice trembled. "He's having another panic attack..." Pedro calmly replied— "Don't worry." "It'll pass."

Then... silence. A few moments later... both of them stepped outside. Merissa's eyes were filled with tears. She whispered— "I shouldn't have let Florence leave like that..." "...If only I had stopped her..." She couldn't finish the sentence.

Floyy slowly looked toward Pedro. For just a second... he saw it. A smile. Not relief. Not sadness. A smile that didn't belong there. A smile that made no sense. A smile... that frightened him.

Floyy quietly closed the notebook. Jenn remained completely still. His fists slowly tightened.

"...Florence..." "...is dead." "And Pedro..." "...didn't even look afraid?"

His voice had become colder than ever. Floyy slid another page across the table.

"I wrote one more thing."

Jenn began reading.

— FROM THE NOTEBOOK —

From today... I will never go back to Peter's house. Because every kindness hides something... Every silence hides something... And behind every smile... There is always... Hidden Malice.

Jenn stopped reading. His eyes remained fixed on those two words. Hidden Malice. He whispered— "...What are you trying to tell me..." "...Floyy?"

Later That Night

Rain had finally stopped. Jenn walked toward a small convenience store. Not because he needed groceries. Only cigarettes.

He stepped inside. Then stopped. Behind the counter... Floyy. The boy quietly worked there after college.

Floyy smiled. "The usual brand, Professor?" Jenn nodded. After paying... he looked at him. "...Do you have a few minutes?"

Both of them sat outside the shop. An old wooden bench overlooked the empty street. Jenn finally broke the silence.

"That phrase..." "'Hidden Malice.'" "What does it really mean?" "You ended your assignment there." "...Doesn't that bother you?"

Floyy looked toward the road. "I came here to learn writing." "I want to continue."

Jenn slowly lit his cigarette. "This isn't about literature anymore." He inhaled deeply. "No matter how close Pedro once was to me..." "...he isn't a stranger." "If something is wrong..." "...I have to know."

Floyy quietly looked at him. "It doesn't matter to me whether Mr. Pedro is a good man..." "...or a bad one." "I'm only here..." "...because I'm learning from you."

Jenn suddenly straightened himself. The hesitation... was gone. His voice became firm.

"Then we'll find the truth." He looked directly at Floyy. "But listen carefully." "If you tell Pedro..." "...anything we've discussed..." "Everything ends." "Our classes." "Our investigation." "Everything."

Floyy nodded slowly. "Tell me what I have to do."

Jenn extinguished the cigarette beneath his shoe. "The first thing..." "...is to find out." "What really happened inside Hotel Bellavista that night."

Floyy frowned. "...You mean..." "I have to go back there?"

Jenn finally smiled. Now... "You understand." He picked up Floyy's notebook. And handed it back.

"This..." "...is your next assignment." He paused. Then quietly said—

"Your first investigation begins now."

Case File Suspended...
